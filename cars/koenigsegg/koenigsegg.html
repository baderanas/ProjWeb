Many 22-year-olds dream of supercars. But Christian von Koenigsegg took matters into his own hands by founding a company to build one. Before his 30th birthday, Koenigsegg cars unleashed one of the world’s fastest and most astonishing models.
By infusing patented technology and refusing to accept anything but perfection, Koenigsegg cars quickly proved their ferocity and superiority. They demolished top speed records, grabbed global headlines, and continue to push the envelope of automotive design.

2024 Koenigsegg CC850

The CC850 sits at 1hp:1kg by pumping out a stomach-churning 1,385 horsepower on its featherlight curb weight of 1,385 kg (3,053 lbs.). It also throws in 1,385 Nm (1,021 lb-ft) of torque for good measure
Engine: 5.0L twin-turbo V-8
0-60 mph: 3 sec (est.)

Buy for: $3.65M Rent for: $8,000/h

2024 Koenigsegg Gemera

Koenigsegg Gemera shifts the paradigm. Despite cranking out a massive 1,700 horsepower, it still has room for you and three of your closest friends. It removed the oversized combustion engine and instead uses three electric motors combined with a compact 600-horsepower combustion Koenigsegg Gemera engine. The Koenigsegg Gemera is a four-seater megacar ready to go for a double date like no other. It also happens to have a 1,000-km (621-mile) combined range. Koenigsegg plans to produce 300 units of the Gemera, the most out of the Koenigsegg factory for a single model.

Buy for: $1.7M Rent for: $4,000/h

2021 Koenigsegg Jesko

The Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut, a low-drag variant of the Jesko, held the title of the fastest car in the world, reaching speeds up to 330 mph (531 km/h). And it does so on a platform that isn’t just great to look at but is also full of interior creature comforts like climate control, high-quality stereo with AirPlay, and more legroom than most other supercars. The Koenigsegg Jesko feeds 20 bar (290 psi) pressurized air into the turbos to reduce lag, features an all-new Light Speed Transmission, and uses three fuel injectors per cylinder to maximize performance. It’s no wonder it received Top Gear’s 2022 Hypercar of the Year award.

Buy for: $3M Rent for: $7,500/h

2016

Koenigsegg founder Christian von Koenigsegg drove the Tesla Model S, he was so impressed by the instant electric torque, it led to the first partially-electric Koenigsegg car.
The Koenigsegg Regera, which means to reign, features three electric motors paired with a 5.0L V-8, creating a combined 1,500 horsepower. This stunning model takes its name literally, ruling over the record for 0–249–0 mph (0–400–0 km/h) at just 31.49 seconds.

Buy for: $1.5M Rent for: $4,000/h

2010 Koenigsegg Agera

Although newer models have surpassed the achievements, the Koenigsegg Agera car firmly secured its place in the history books. It was Top Gear’s 2010 Hypercar of the Year and continued to achieve greatness for many years after.
The Koenigsegg Agera R broke six world records in one incredible session in 2011, including 0–186–0 mph (0–300–0 km/h) in only 21.19 seconds. The 2015 improvements were released as the Agera RS, which holds a record-setting top speed of 277.87 mph (447.19 km/h).

Buy for: $1.5M Rent for: $3,500/h

2006 Koenigsegg CCX

As a rear-wheel drive model with a 6-speed manual gearbox and dual superchargers, the Koenigsegg CCX is undeniably from a bygone era. Yet it remains a stunning example of hypercar performance and style. Koenigsegg produced 29 units of the original CCX, then continued forward with no less than six variants. The Koenigsegg CCXR incorporated more eco-friendly technology to use ethanol fuel, while the CCX Edition reduced weight for ultimate track performance.

Buy for: $700,000 Rent for: $2,500/h
